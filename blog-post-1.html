5 quick ways to avoid a lockout

Jan 31, 2026 • 3 min read

Lockouts happen fast: you step out “for a second”, the door clicks, and suddenly your keys are on the wrong side. Here are a few simple habits that prevent most lockouts.

1) Make a “keys-phone-wallet” pause

Before closing the door, take one second to check pockets or bag. This tiny habit prevents the majority of accidental lockouts.

2) Keep a spare key in a smart place

Avoid obvious hiding spots. A trusted neighbour, friend, or a secure key box is often a better option than “under the mat”.

3) Fix sticky locks early

If your key sticks, turns roughly, or the latch doesn’t align, it’s a warning sign. Small adjustments and repairs now can prevent a full failure later.

4) Consider a smart lock (if it fits your door)

Smart locks can reduce lockouts, but they still need good alignment and compatible hardware. If you’re unsure, ask for a quick compatibility check.

5) Know what to do if you’re already locked out

Don’t force the door. If you can’t safely access a spare key, call a locksmith. We aim for non-destructive entry first and confirm pricing before work begins.
